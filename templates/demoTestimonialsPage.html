About the Developer

The story behind NeuralDrive and why it was built.

Devin Pattison

Developer of NeuralDrive

Connect on LinkedIn

My name is Devin Pattison. I’m a software engineer with a background in computer science, mathematics, and data systems. The reason I built this platform didn’t start as a startup idea — it started from real work I was doing with businesses that needed automation.

A few years ago in West Palm Beach, I started building automated workflows for professional service companies. Law firms, service providers, and other businesses needed ways to automate repetitive processes like lead intake, follow-ups, scheduling, and CRM management. Using tools like API workflows, automation platforms, and backend systems, I helped companies eliminate hours of manual work and improve how they handled incoming leads.

That experience showed me something important: automation can dramatically improve operations, but there are certain industries where you cannot simply replace people with AI. One of the clearest examples of that was sales.

Sales — especially in industries like automotive — depends heavily on human connection. Confidence, tone, energy, personality, and timing matter. Customers don’t want to talk to robots when they’re making a large purchase like a vehicle. So the question became: how can AI improve sales performance without replacing the human element?

That question led to the idea behind this platform.

Instead of trying to replace salespeople, the idea was to use AI to train them.

I began developing this application while working with a dealership organization. I had the opportunity to work alongside the second largest Toyota dealership in the United States, which gave me real exposure to how high-performing sales teams actually operate. Watching their team interact with customers, handle objections, and manage follow-up conversations gave me a much deeper understanding of the challenges sales managers face when trying to train new reps.

One of the biggest problems was repetition. The best salespeople had thousands of conversations worth of experience, while newer reps simply hadn't had enough real interactions yet. Managers can roleplay with reps occasionally, but they rarely have time to run dozens of practice conversations every week.

So I built a system where reps could practice those conversations with an AI voice agent that behaves like a real customer.

The results were fascinating.

When sales reps began using the system, the experienced salespeople consistently scored higher — exactly what you would expect. But something interesting happened with the newer reps. As they kept practicing conversations, their scores began improving. You could literally see their confidence growing.

What surprised me the most was how the reps reacted to the system.

It almost felt like a game to them.

They wanted to run another scenario. Then another one. They compared scores with each other. Some of them tried to beat their previous performance like it was a leaderboard.

You could see their faces light up when they handled a tough objection correctly or improved their score. That was the moment I realized this idea had real potential — not just as a tool, but as a new way to make training engaging.

NeuralDrive was built around a simple philosophy: use AI to help salespeople become better humans at selling.

The goal isn’t to remove personality, spirit, or human connection from sales. The goal is to give people the experience, repetition, and feedback they need to develop those skills faster.

This platform is the result of that idea.
